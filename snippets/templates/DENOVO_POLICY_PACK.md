## 3. FAQ CONTENT

### Q: Can I use Build and Launch together?
Yes. You can generate with Build, then add Launch for any app you want
hosted and operated by DeNovo.

### Q: What is included in Build?
Build includes application generation and source code delivery. It does not
include ongoing hosting, outbound communications, third-party vendor fees,
or managed compliance unless expressly stated.

### Q: What is included in Launch?
Launch includes standard hosting, monitoring, backups, and routine operational
support for ordinary commercial apps.

### Q: Are email and SMS included?
Not as unlimited services. Email, SMS, and similar communication services may
be quota-limited, customer-supplied, or billed separately depending on plan
and workload.

### Q: Are storage and bandwidth included?
Standard plans include only ordinary operational usage. Higher-volume storage,
bandwidth, webhook traffic, and similar variable-cost services may be billed
separately or require an upgrade.

### Q: What happens if I hit my limit?
Additional builds, hosted apps, or metered services are billed at the applicable
overage rate or may require a plan change.

### Q: Do unused allocations roll over?
Monthly allocations reset each billing cycle. Annual plans receive their annual
allocation across the term.

### Q: Who owns the code?
For Build plans, you own the delivered codebase, subject to DeNovo's
pre-existing tooling, templates, methods, and platform materials.

### Q: Who owns the domain and provider accounts?
Customers are generally expected to own business-critical assets such as
domains, payment processor accounts, and certain third-party providers.

### Q: Can I run a healthcare or financial app on Standard Launch?
Not by default. Regulated or high-compliance workloads require separate review,
scope, and contract terms.

### Q: Can DeNovo suspend or restrict service?
Yes. DeNovo may throttle, suspend, or require plan changes where usage creates
abuse risk, abnormal cost, reputational harm, or compliance exposure.

## 5. MSA / TERMS PAGE CONTENT

### 5.1 Launch Scope and Exclusions

Standard Launch includes hosting, monitoring, backups, and routine operational
support for ordinary commercial software workloads.

Standard Launch does not include, and Customer may not use Standard Launch for,
unless expressly agreed in writing:

- regulated healthcare workloads involving protected health information
- regulated financial, insurance, lending, custody, advisory, or money
  transmission workflows
- custom data residency, retention, audit, or compliance obligations
- dedicated or isolated infrastructure
- unlimited or unmetered email, SMS, storage, bandwidth, webhooks, or
  third-party API usage
- custom legal, regulatory, or compliance review
- unlawful, deceptive, abusive, or high-risk use cases as determined by
  DeNovo acting reasonably

DeNovo may suspend, restrict, reclassify, or require migration of any workload
that exceeds the operational, legal, security, or compliance scope of Standard
Launch.

---

### 5.2 Ancillary Services and Billing

Certain services associated with a generated, hosted, or operated application
may be billed separately from Build and Launch fees. Such services may include:

- transactional email
- SMS or messaging services
- file storage
- bandwidth or egress
- webhook volume
- external API relay or proxy usage
- premium backups
- premium monitoring or SLA coverage
- third-party software and infrastructure fees

Ancillary services may be subject to quotas, metered usage, pass-through
charges, or overage fees.

DeNovo may throttle, suspend, or require an upgrade, migration, or alternate
provider configuration where ancillary usage materially exceeds standard plan
assumptions or creates disproportionate operational, reputational, compliance,
or financial risk.

---

### 5.3 Acceptable Use

Customer shall not use the Services or any application generated, deployed, or
operated through the Services to:

- transmit spam, malware, or abusive communications
- host unlawful, infringing, deceptive, or harmful content
- facilitate fraud, impersonation, harassment, or unauthorized surveillance
- circumvent quotas, rate limits, billing controls, or technical protections
- interfere with DeNovo systems, shared infrastructure, or other customer
  environments
- use shared services in a manner that harms sender reputation, degrades
  service quality, or creates abnormal operational risk
- process regulated data or operate regulated workflows outside the scope of
  Customer's contracted plan

DeNovo may investigate suspected misuse and may suspend, throttle, remove
content, disable functionality, or terminate access where reasonably necessary
to protect DeNovo, its providers, other customers, or the public.

---

### 5.4 Ownership and Intellectual Property

Subject to Customer's payment of all amounts due, Customer owns the specific
code delivered to Customer through Build plans, excluding DeNovo's pre-existing
intellectual property, including without limitation DeNovo's platform materials,
generalized templates, tooling, orchestration systems, methods, know-how, and
infrastructure. DeNovo retains all right, title, and interest in and to its
pre-existing and independently developed materials, systems, and methods.

Customer retains ownership of its business data, domains, branding assets, and
customer-facing content.

For Launch plans:
- infrastructure components may be customer-owned or DeNovo-managed depending
  on the applicable configuration
- Customer may be required to maintain customer-owned domains, payment processor
  accounts, email providers, messaging providers, databases, or external APIs
- DeNovo does not transfer ownership of its internal tooling, deployment methods,
  generalized templates, or platform infrastructure except as expressly agreed

### 5.5 Compliance Disclaimer

DeNovo provides software generation, hosting, and operational services and does
not provide legal, tax, accounting, regulatory, security certification, or
compliance advice.

Customer is solely responsible for:
- determining whether its use case is subject to applicable laws or regulations
- obtaining appropriate legal or regulatory advice
- reviewing generated policies and disclosures
- ensuring that its actual business practices match the representations made in
  its applications and customer communications

Unless expressly agreed in writing, Standard plans are not intended for regulated
healthcare, financial, insurance, money transmission, or other high-compliance
use cases.

---

### 5.6 BYO Provider Policy

DeNovo may require that certain third-party services be provisioned, owned, or
controlled by Customer, including domains, payment processors, transactional
email providers, messaging providers, databases, storage services, and external
APIs.

DeNovo may also require migration from DeNovo-managed services to
Customer-owned services where usage, abuse risk, compliance burden, reputational
exposure, or variable cost materially exceeds standard plan assumptions.

**Default ownership expectations:**

Always customer-owned:
- primary domain
- payment processor / merchant account
- brand assets

Customer-owned preferred:
- transactional email provider
- messaging / SMS provider
- database project
- external API vendors with recurring usage costs

DeNovo-managed within quotas (starter/small plans only):
- email relay (within monthly quota)
- standard monitoring
- standard backups
- low-volume shared services

---

### 5.7 Suspension and Protection Rights

DeNovo may implement quotas, caps, throttles, suspensions, or plan changes
where reasonably necessary to:

- prevent abuse
- contain cost exposure
- preserve shared infrastructure
- protect sender reputation
- satisfy provider requirements
- reduce legal, security, or compliance risk

services may need to be configured in your own accounts.

**Build**

- Standard product structure

**Launch**
- Standard hosting
- Monitoring

quota-limited, or billed separately:
- Transactional email

- Storage
- Bandwidth
- Premium backups
- Premium support
- External APIs and integrations

Customers are generally expected to own or control:
- Domain names
- Payment processor accounts
- Brand assets

Standard plans are designed for ordinary commercial workloads. If your app
touches regulated healthcare, financial, insurance, money movement, or other
high-compliance workflows, tell us before launch. Those use cases may require
different scope, pricing, controls, and contract terms.

## 7. GENERATED LEGAL SCAFFOLDS

The following are starter templates DeNovo generates for each app.
These are templates only — not legal advice. Customer must review and approve.

### Privacy Policy scaffold
Covers: data collected, how it is used, third-party services, user rights,
contact information. Customer must review for jurisdiction and business model.

### Terms of Service scaffold
Covers: service description, user obligations, payment terms, termination,
limitation of liability, governing law. Customer must review and approve.

### Cookie Notice scaffold
Covers: cookie types used, consent mechanism, opt-out instructions.
Customer must review for jurisdiction (GDPR, CCPA, etc.).

### Acceptable Use Policy scaffold
Covers: prohibited uses, enforcement rights, user content standards.
Customer may extend for their specific platform type.

### DMCA / Contact scaffold
Covers: designated DMCA agent, takedown process, counter-notice process.

**Important:** DeNovo provides scaffolds, not legal advice.
These must be reviewed by a qualified attorney before use in regulated,
high-risk, or complex commercial contexts.
